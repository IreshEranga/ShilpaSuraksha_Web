Literature Survey

Academic risk identification for primary students remains a largely reactive, manual process in Sri Lanka. Our research synthesizes global findings on early warning systems, behavioral analytics, and adaptive learning to address this critical gap.

Early Warning Systems in Education

Studies show that early identification of at-risk students using attendance, academic performance, and behavioral data can reduce dropout rates by up to 30%. Systems that flag students early allow timely teacher intervention before academic gaps widen significantly.

Behavioral & Emotional Analytics

Research in child psychology and educational neuroscience confirms that handwriting analysis, drawing patterns, and behavioral cues are reliable indicators of a child's emotional and cognitive state. CNN-based models have demonstrated 85%+ accuracy in classifying emotional states from drawings.

Personalized Learning Paths

Adaptive learning systems tailored to individual weaknesses demonstrate superior outcomes over static curricula. By utilizing a Hybrid AI architecture—combining Natural Language Processing (NLP) and Graph Neural Networks (GNN) for prerequisite mapping—this approach overcomes the limitations of traditional Collaborative Filtering. Integrated with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), the system delivers personalized, factually grounded, and culturally relevant educational interventions.

Student Clustering & Dashboards

Machine learning clustering techniques (K-Means, DBSCAN) applied to academic datasets enable teachers to visualize performance distributions and intervene at the group level. Interactive dashboards combining academic and behavioral metrics improve teacher decision-making efficiency.

Research Gap

What's Missing

Existing solutions fail to address the multi-dimensional nature of academic risk in Sri Lankan primary school contexts.

🔗

No Unified Multi-Dimensional System

Existing educational systems in Sri Lanka address only academic data (marks, attendance) in isolation. There is no integrated system that combines academic performance, emotional state, behavioral patterns, and intelligent intervention into one platform for primary school teachers.

🌏

Lack of Local Context

International EdTech solutions are not adapted for the Sri Lankan primary school curriculum, grading structures, or Sinhala language contexts. There is an acute absence of locally-trained ML models for Sri Lankan student behavioral data.

⏱️

Reactive Rather Than Predictive

Current school management systems (if any) are purely administrative. They do not predict academic risk — teachers identify struggling students only after grades deteriorate, leaving little time for effective intervention before assessments.

🗺️

No Automated Learning Path Generation

While commercial LMS platforms exist, none automatically generate personalized remedial learning paths for primary students based on specific subject weaknesses identified via AI analysis, especially for the Sri Lankan curriculum framework.

Research Problem & Proposed Solution

The Core Problem

Primary school teachers in Sri Lanka manage large classrooms with limited tools. By the time academic decline is noticed, critical learning milestones have already been missed.

🔍

Proposed Research Problem

How can an integrated AI-driven system identify academically at-risk primary school students in Sri Lanka through multi-dimensional analysis — combining academic performance data, emotional state detection from handwriting and drawings, and behavioral patterns — to provide teachers with timely, actionable, and personalized intervention strategies?

By the time a student's academic decline is noticed, critical learning milestones have already been missed. The problem compounds when emotional and behavioral factors go undetected.

🛡️

Proposed Solution — ShilpaSuraksha

ShilpaSuraksha (Shield of Protection) is a full-stack web platform that integrates four AI-powered modules under a unified teacher interface. School administrators manage schools and teachers; teachers manage students, feed academic and behavioral data, and receive AI-generated risk flags and personalized learning recommendations.

The system uses trained ML models for academic risk prediction and behavioral/emotion analysis, an intelligent recommendation engine for learning path generation, and a real-time dashboard for student progress visualization and intervention tracking.

Research Objectives

What We Set Out to Achieve

Four focused objectives drive the ShilpaSuraksha research, each corresponding to a dedicated system component.

01

Develop an Academic Early Warning System

Build a machine learning model that analyzes student attendance records and assessment marks to predict academic risk levels (weak/not weak), enabling proactive teacher intervention before irreversible learning gaps form.

02

Create a Behavioral and Emotional Analysis Module

Design and train a deep learning model (CNN-based) capable of analyzing student hand drawings and writings to detect emotional distress and behavioral anomalies that correlate with academic struggles.

03

Build an Intelligent Personalized Recommendation System

Develop an AI recommendation engine that generates tailored learning paths (activities, assessments, tasks) for identified at-risk students based on their specific subject weaknesses and weak sections, tracked progressively over time.

04

Design an Interactive Student Improvement Dashboard

Create a comprehensive teacher-facing dashboard that clusters students by academic and behavioral risk profiles, visualizes progress trends, orchestrates interventions, and provides outcome summaries to support data-driven teaching decisions.

Methodology

System Architecture & Approach

ShilpaSuraksha follows a layered architecture with a React frontend, FastAPI backend, PostgreSQL database, and trained ML/DL models served via dedicated model APIs.

📥

Data Collection

Teachers input academic marks, attendance, and upload behavioral samples (drawings/handwriting) per student.

🤖

AI Analysis

Trained ML models process academic data; CNN models analyze behavioral inputs to generate risk predictions.

🗂️

Recommendation

At-risk students receive auto-generated personalized learning paths with activities and assessments aligned to weak areas.

📊

Dashboard & Tracking

Teachers monitor clusters, interventions, and progress through an interactive analytics dashboard with trend visualization.

Component 01

Early Warning System

Accepts teacher-entered attendance and assessment marks for each student. A trained classification model processes this academic data to label students as academically weak or on track. The system surfaces at-risk students with supporting data visualizations.

IR Iresh — Academic Data & ML Model
Component 02

Emotion & Behavioral Analysis Module

Teachers upload student hand drawings and handwriting samples. A trained CNN-based deep learning model classifies behavioral and emotional patterns from these visual inputs to flag students showing signs of emotional distress or learning difficulties.

SH Shenori — Behavioral Data & CNN Model
Component 03

Intelligent Recommendation System

Displays consolidated lists of academically and behaviorally at-risk students. Teachers specify the weak subject and section for each student, and the system auto-generates a personalized learning path — activities, tasks, and assessments — with progress tracking over time.

KV Kavini — Learning Path Generation
Component 04

Student Improvement Dashboard with Intervention Orchestrator

A rich analytics dashboard that clusters students by academic, behavioral, emotional, and risk indicators using ML clustering. Provides trend charts, individual vs. class benchmarks, a unified intervention panel with active/recommended activities, delivery history, and outcome summaries.

SA Sahan — Dashboard & Clustering

System Architecture Overview

Frontend

React.js based dashboard providing intuitive interfaces for teachers and administrators to view risk analytics and intervention roadmaps.

Backend API

Node.js & Express RESTful APIs handling role-based access control (RBAC), data processing, and routing to AI/ML services.

Hybrid AI & ML Layer

Integrates Graph Neural Networks (GNN), custom NLP (TF-IDF), Google Gemini via RAG architecture, and Bayesian probabilistic forecasting.

Database

PostgreSQL managing student records, attendance, behavioral indicators, historical assessment data, and localized curriculum resources.
